FAQ

What is the main difference between a hydraulic and mechanical jack?

The primary difference between a hydraulic and mechanical jack lies in their lifting mechanism. Hydraulic jacks use fluid pressure to lift the vehicle, providing a smoother and more effortless operation. Mechanical jacks, on the other hand, rely on manual effort and a screw-based mechanism to lift the vehicle.

Can I use an electric jack with my vehicle's battery?

Yes, most electric jacks are designed to be used with a vehicle's 12V battery. However, it's essential to ensure that your vehicle's battery is in good condition and can handle the power requirements of the electric jack. Additionally, always follow the manufacturer's instructions for safe and proper use.

What safety precautions should I take when using a jack?

When using a jack, it's crucial to follow basic safety guidelines to avoid accidents and injuries. Always choose a level surface, ensure the vehicle is in park or neutral gear, and apply the parking brake. Use jack stands for added support and never get under the vehicle while it's supported by a jack alone.
